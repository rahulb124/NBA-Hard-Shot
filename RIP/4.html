Why having encrypted devices are important in a growing technology industry

Encryption is very important in our growing technology industry because our data is held in more places online than ever before, and the lack of strong encryption could result in a massive disaster in which people would not be able to access critical information.

The United States government holds the most amount of encrypted data about American citizens. However, what the FBI is failing to realize, is that if the FBI requires insecurities in tech companies products that much of the goverment uses, it can allow hackers to gain access to the goverment's data, which includes the data of highly classified material as well as it's citizens data.

The government already had the right to compelled decryption in which the U.S. Justice Department can force an owner to unlock their device, or decrypt in another possible way. According to the Third Circuit, individuals can be sent to prison if they do not obey a court order and can be held until they complete the court order, which in this case could be unlocking an iPhone. The difference here is that the government was warrant a decryption, whereas the FBI Backdoor could possibly be done without the user even knowing.
